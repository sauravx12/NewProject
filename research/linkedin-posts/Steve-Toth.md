"Google AI Overviews are 90% accurate."

That's from a recent New York Times headline.

Here's what it doesn't tell you:

The Times tested 4,326 Google searches with AI startup Oumi.

At Google's scale, 90% accuracy means:

- Hundreds of thousands of wrong answers per minute
- Tens of millions of errors per hour
- Facebook is the 2nd most-cited source
- Reddit is the 4th
- 56% of correct answers cite sources that don't support them
- That sourcing problem got worse — up from 37% with Gemini 2

The accuracy improved.

The verifiability got worse.

More than half the time the answer is right, you can't confirm it from the sources Google links to.

But here's what most brands will miss:

Getting cited is easier than you think.

A journalist wrote a fake blog post claiming he won a hot dog eating contest. Google cited it as fact the next day.

One blog post. One day.

If a fake hot dog championship gets into AI Overviews overnight, imagine what clear, well-structured answers to your customers' actual questions can do.

We see this with clients constantly. The brands producing citable answers to their ICP's deal-breaker questions are getting pulled in. The ones waiting aren't.

The bar for citation isn't high.

The bar for accurate, trustworthy citation is.

And we see too many brands not yet clearing it.
