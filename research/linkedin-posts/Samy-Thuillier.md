The last Google update was a bloodbath (again). Every time Google drops an update the same movie plays out:

Panic, publish more content, build emergency links, blame the algorithm, switch agencies, wait and hope it reverses... then report the traffic losses to leadership without a single hypothesis about why it happened.

None of that is a strategy. 
It's just anxiety with a Slack channel.

The teams that recover fastest do the opposite of what feels urgent: 

- They stop publishing and audit what they already have. 
- They ask why their existing links didn't protect them before buying more. 
- They READ the actual update documentation and take it literally instead of waiting for a Twitter thread to summarize it for them. 
- They treat every update as permanent until proven otherwise, because most of them are.

The biggest one: when they report to leadership, they don't just say traffic dropped. 

They say which pages dropped, by how much, and here's my hypothesis about why. 

That's the difference between an SEO team that gets cut in the next round and one that gets more budget.

Maybe you didn't think about that one: Substack.
